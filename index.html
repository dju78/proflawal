1. Vision Statement

To position the UBA Professorial Chair in Finance as a national and continental centre of excellence in finance, financial economics, macro-financial policy research, innovation, academic capacity development, and policy engagement, advancing cutting-edge scholarship and practical financial solutions that strengthen financial systems, corporate governance, sustainability, financial technology, and macro-financial stability in Nigeria and Africa.

2. Areas of Academic and Research Competence of the Chair

The Chair will leverage established expertise in the following areas to drive research, teaching, industry collaboration, and policy engagement:

Finance and Financial Economics

Core theoretical and empirical foundations for financial research and innovation

Macroeconomics and Macro-Financial Policy

Policy frameworks for financial stability and economic development

Corporate Governance and Firm Performance

Governance structures and their impact on organizational outcomes

Banking Stability and Risk Management

Risk assessment and mitigation strategies for financial institutions

Sustainable Finance, ESG, and CSR

Environmental, social, and governance integration in finance

Financial Technology (FinTech), Blockchain, and Digital Finance

Emerging technologies transforming financial services

Econometrics and Advanced Quantitative Methods

Statistical and computational tools for finance research

These competence areas provide the theoretical, empirical, and policy foundations for the innovative products, processes, and academic programmes to be developed under the Chair.

3. Core Goals of the Chair

  1. Advance high-impact research in finance, financial economics, macro-financial stability, governance, sustainability, and financial technology.
  2. Collaborate with the Donor (UBA) to develop and support innovative financial products and processes aligned with emerging technologies such as blockchain, cryptocurrency, and FinTech.
  3. Initiate and champion programmes, research activities, and academic engagements that strengthen academic development within the Department, Faculty, and University.
  4. Build strong research capacity among staff and postgraduate students.
  5. Bridge academia, industry, policy, and the Donor through structured engagements.
  6. Modernise postgraduate training and curriculum in line with global developments in finance and macro-financial analysis.
  7. Enhance institutional and donor visibility through global collaborations, conferences, and publications.

Key Behavioral Trends in 2025

  • Transaction Leadership: Gen Z and Millennials are responsible for the vast majority of instant payment adoption in Nigeria
  • Digital Dominance: 99% of Gen Z reported using a mobile banking app in the last month, with nearly 50% preferring to manage their finances exclusively via smartphone
  • Investment Activity: Retail trading driven largely by younger investors makes up nearly one-third of the total trade value on the Nigerian Exchange
  • Savings Culture: 68% of Gen Z consumers now save at least 5% of their monthly income
  • Crypto Preference: Approximately 42% of Gen Z investors in Nigeria have invested in cryptocurrency

Strategic Outlook for 2026

Traditional banks like UBA are under intense pressure to modernize. In February 2026, industry experts emphasized that heavy investment in digital infrastructure is no longer optional but a "standard expectation" to retain a customer base that is now almost entirely digital-native.

Strategic Recommendation: UBA Pulse

A hybrid strategy where UBA uses a "sub-brand" to target Gen Z without changing its main corporate image. This "Two-Speed Brand" architecture allows UBA to protect its corporate reputation while aggressively capturing the Gen Z market.

üöÄ UBA Pulse - The Sub-Brand Approach

Tagline: "Your Vibe. Your Money. Your Future."

Positioning: A vibrant, mobile-only interface that looks like a fintech app (similar to Kuda or OPay), using informal language, dark-mode-first design, and rewards users with lifestyle perks.

Backend: Powered by UBA's 2026 infrastructure, maintaining stability and trust while delivering fintech-level speed.

Development Approach

For an enterprise-grade app capable of handling millions of transactions with AI-driven features, the development will be split across technical tiers:

MVP Development

Core wallet, instant transfer, basic UI

Enterprise Platform

Scalable backend, multi-region support, legacy integration

AI Features

Personalization, fraud detection, AI chatbots (like Leo)

Security & Compliance

Biometrics, encryption, NDPR/GDPR compliance

Timeline: A competitive app following digital-first standards requires 9 to 18 months of development, with phased rollout across MVP, Enterprise, and Advanced features.

6. Expected Impact of the Chair

  • Strong alignment between academic research and UBA's innovation objectives
  • Contribution to macro-financial policy discussions and evidence-based recommendations for financial sector stability
  • Enhanced research reputation of the Department, Faculty, and University
  • Development of expertise in emerging financial technologies
  • Production of highly skilled postgraduate researchers
  • Attraction of funding, partnerships, and global recognition
  • Sustainable academic and industry legacy beyond the tenure of the Chair

Conclusion

The UBA Professorial Chair in Finance will function as a platform for transformative research, financial innovation, macro-financial policy engagement, academic leadership, and structured industry collaboration, significantly advancing finance scholarship and practice while enhancing the strategic visibility and impact of the Donor, the Department, and the University.
